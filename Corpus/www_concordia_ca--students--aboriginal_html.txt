Aboriginal students
Aboriginal students
The Aboriginal Student Resource Centre is an on-campus resource for First Nations, Métis and Inuit students. We help students succeed and achieve their highest academic potential by working with them to make the most of the many resources available at the university.
Featured News
Valedictorian Shiann Whitebean
At the June 5 Arts and Science convocation ceremony, the valedictorian address was given by Shiann Whitebean, a Wolf Clan member of the Kanien’kehá:ka (Mohawk) Nation at Kahnawà:ke.
Resources and services
The ASRC is your space where you will discover a community within the university! A welcoming location in downtown Montreal where students can meet, plan social activities or relax between classes.  We are a walk-in centre for future and current students. We will try our best to help you on the spot, or schedule a follow-up appointment.
Coming to Concordia
Whether you are applying to Concordia, or just starting out here, you can find your home away from home at Concordia's . First Nations, Métis and Inuit students are supported by many programs. Come drop in to our resource Centre at our thriving downtown location.

A message from MNA Kelley
On the occasion of Convocation, the Quebec minister responsible for Native Affairs, Geoffrey Kelley, delivered a message to all of Concordia's graduating Aboriginal students and congratulated the ACSD for providing to Métis, First Nations and Inuit students "a place of their own on campus."
