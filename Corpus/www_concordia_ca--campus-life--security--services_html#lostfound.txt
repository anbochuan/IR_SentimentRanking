Services
The Concordia University Security Department offers the following services to the university community, as well as to the general public, on both campuses.
In order to ensure that the university community can count on a secure environment, the Concordia University Security Department constantly patrols its territory, on foot or in a patrol car.
The principle mandate of the Security Agent is to protect the public and its property. They are available to respond to all your questions. Do not hesitate to seek their advice.
The Concordia University Security Department offers members of the community an accompanying service on the two campuses, at all times.
This service is subject to assessment and to certain restrictions.
If you feel you are being pursued, harassed or threatened by one or several individuals, on campus contact Concordia  Services
Internal phone dial 3717 option 1
External phone dial 514-848-3717 option 1
If you have dialed 911 , don't forget to call Concordia Security Services.
The Concordia University Security Department provides a service for articles that are lost or found, according to the University’s policy, .
Lost and found articles may be reported to one of the following service offices:
Sir George Williams Campus
Hall Building: Room H 118
John Molson School of Business: Room GM 199-10
McConnell Building: Room LB 121
Visual Arts Building: Room VA 111
Faubourg Tower: Room FB 099
Engineering, Computer Science and Visual Arts Integrated Complex: RoomEV 1.402
Grey Nuns Residence: GN Room J 104
Loyola Campus
Administration Building: Room AD 220
Science Complex: Rooms SP 144 and SP 170
For lost items, please present yourself to one of our service desks mentioned above.
The Concordia University Security Department offers an investigation service for all infractions committed contrary to the policies, regulations and codes in effect at the university.
Furthermore, the investigative service works in collaboration with several police divisions and external service providers.
This service analyzes the current trends and assesses the risk and threat levels. It puts in place preventative measures and the necessary corrective measures to ensure that our community and our community’s property are safe.
