Immigration guide for faculty
Moving to Montreal to start work at Concordia? From work permits and insurance, to taxes and frequently asked moving questions, we’ve got the information you need.
Moving to Canada
For information on moving your goods to Canada, consult .
Setting up at Concordia

Work permits
Individuals who are neither Canadian citizens nor Canadian permanent residents must obtain a  to be allowed to work at Concordia University. We cannot pay anyone who is not legally entitled to work in Canada.
Social insurance numbers
Once in Canada, individuals must apply for a temporary  at the branch office of Service Canada that serves their local area of residence.
Health insurance
Individuals who are new to Québec should apply for  as soon as possible as there is a 3-month post-registration waiting period.
Income taxes
As soon as individuals commence employment, the appropriate  will be deducted. The Québec government offers a  for up to five years for foreign-national and repatriated Canadian professors in designated disciplines.
Canadian permanent residency
In Québec, all applications for  status follow a two-step process: acquiring a certificat de sélection du Québec (CSQ) from the Province of Québec and getting federal approval from Citizenship and Immigration Canada.
